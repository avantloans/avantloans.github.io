Best Personal Loans for Home Improvement Projects

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Home improvement projects are more popular than ever, especially as people continue to spend more time at home. Whether you're renovating your kitchen, adding a home office, or upgrading your backyard, financing these projects can be a challenge. Personal loans offer a flexible solution, allowing homeowners to borrow money without using their property as collateral. In this guide, we’ll explore the best personal loans for home improvement, factors to consider when choosing one, and how to maximize your borrowing power.

Why Choose a Personal Loan for Home Improvements?

Unlike home equity loans or HELOCs (Home Equity Lines of Credit), personal loans don’t require you to put your home on the line. This makes them an attractive option for homeowners who:
- Don’t have enough equity in their homes
- Want to avoid lengthy approval processes
- Prefer fixed repayment terms

Additionally, personal loans often have faster funding times—sometimes within a day—which is ideal for urgent renovations.

Top Lenders for Home Improvement Personal Loans

1. SoFi

Best for Low Interest Rates
SoFi offers competitive APRs and no origination fees, making it a great choice for borrowers with strong credit (typically 680+). Their loans range from $5,000 to $100,000, with flexible repayment terms.

Key Features:
- Unemployment protection (payment pauses available)
- Member perks like career coaching

2. LightStream

Best for Large Projects
LightStream provides loans up to $100,000 with some of the lowest interest rates in the market. They also offer a rate-beat program, where they’ll beat a competitor’s rate by 0.10%.

Key Features:
- No fees (no origination, prepayment, or late fees)
- Same-day funding available

3. Upstart

Best for Fair Credit Borrowers
Upstart uses AI to evaluate borrowers, meaning even those with limited credit history (but strong income potential) may qualify. APRs start as low as 5.20%, with loan amounts from $1,000 to $50,000.

Key Features:
- Fast approval process
- Accepts credit scores as low as 300

4. Discover

Best for Debt Consolidation
If you’re financing home improvements while consolidating high-interest debt, Discover offers loans up to $40,000 with fixed rates and no origination fees.

Key Features:
- Direct payment to creditors (for debt consolidation)
- 30-day money-back guarantee

5. Avant

Best for Bad Credit
Avant specializes in borrowers with lower credit scores (600+). While their APRs are higher, they provide accessibility for those who might not qualify elsewhere.

Key Features:
- Flexible repayment options
- Funds available as soon as the next business day

How to Choose the Right Loan

1. Check Your Credit Score

Your credit score will heavily influence your APR. Generally:
- 720+: Best rates
- 650-719: Moderate rates
- Below 650: Higher rates or fewer options

2. Compare Interest Rates and Fees

Look beyond the APR—consider origination fees, prepayment penalties, and late fees. Some lenders (like LightStream) charge no fees at all.

3. Calculate Your Budget

Use a loan calculator to estimate monthly payments. Ensure the loan fits comfortably within your budget to avoid financial strain.

4. Read Reviews and Lender Reputation

Check customer feedback on platforms like Trustpilot or the Better Business Bureau (BBB) to avoid predatory lenders.

Alternative Financing Options

If a personal loan isn’t the right fit, consider:
- Home Equity Loans: Lower rates but require equity and collateral.
- Credit Cards: Best for small projects (0% APR introductory offers can help).
- Government Loans: FHA Title I loans or energy-efficient mortgages may apply.

Final Tips for Borrowing Wisely

  • Get Pre-Qualified: Many lenders offer soft credit checks that don’t affect your score.
  • Negotiate Rates: Some lenders may match competitors’ offers.
  • Plan for Contingencies: Renovations often go over budget—factor in a 10-20% buffer.

By choosing the right personal loan, you can turn your home improvement dreams into reality without unnecessary financial stress. Whether you're upgrading for comfort, sustainability, or increased home value, smart borrowing ensures your project stays on track.
