The Best Student Loan Refinancing Options for Undergraduates

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Student loan debt is a growing crisis in the United States, with over 45 million borrowers collectively owing more than $1.7 trillion. For undergraduates, managing this debt can feel overwhelming, especially when high interest rates and rigid repayment terms make it difficult to stay afloat. Fortunately, student loan refinancing offers a way to reduce monthly payments, lower interest rates, and even shorten repayment terms.

If you're an undergraduate looking to refinance your student loans, this guide will walk you through the best options available today. We’ll cover key lenders, eligibility requirements, and strategies to maximize savings.

Why Refinancing Undergraduate Student Loans Makes Sense

Refinancing replaces your existing loans with a new private loan, ideally at a lower interest rate. For undergraduates, this can be particularly beneficial because:

  • Lower Interest Rates – If your credit score has improved since taking out your loans, you may qualify for better rates.
  • Simplified Payments – Combining multiple loans into one can make repayment easier.
  • Flexible Terms – Choose between shorter terms (to pay off debt faster) or longer terms (to reduce monthly payments).

However, refinancing federal loans with a private lender means losing federal protections like income-driven repayment (IDR) plans and potential loan forgiveness. Weigh the pros and cons carefully before proceeding.

Top Student Loan Refinancing Lenders for Undergraduates

1. SoFi

SoFi is one of the most popular refinancing lenders, offering competitive rates and flexible terms.

Key Features:

  • No origination or prepayment fees
  • Unemployment protection (temporarily pauses payments if you lose your job)
  • Career coaching and financial planning resources

Eligibility:

  • Minimum credit score: 650+
  • Must be a U.S. citizen or permanent resident
  • Requires stable income or a cosigner

2. Earnest

Earnest stands out for its precision pricing model, which customizes rates based on your financial habits.

Key Features:

  • Flexible repayment terms (5 to 20 years)
  • Ability to skip one payment per year
  • No fees

Eligibility:

  • Strong credit history (680+ recommended)
  • Proof of responsible financial behavior (savings, low credit utilization)

3. Laurel Road

Laurel Road caters to professionals and graduates but also offers strong refinancing options for undergraduates with good credit.

Key Features:

  • Competitive fixed and variable rates
  • Autopay discount (0.25% reduction)
  • No application or origination fees

Eligibility:

  • Minimum credit score: 660
  • Must have a degree or be within six months of graduation

4. CommonBond

CommonBond is ideal for borrowers who value social responsibility—they fund education for children in need with every loan.

Key Features:

  • Hybrid repayment options (mix of fixed and variable rates)
  • Three-day grace period for late payments
  • Career support services

Eligibility:

  • Must be a U.S. citizen or green card holder
  • Minimum credit score: 660

How to Choose the Best Refinancing Option

1. Compare Interest Rates

Even a small difference in rates can save thousands over time. Check multiple lenders to find the best deal.

2. Consider Repayment Terms

  • Short-term (5-10 years): Higher monthly payments but less interest paid overall.
  • Long-term (15-20 years): Lower monthly payments but more interest over time.

3. Check for Additional Benefits

Some lenders offer perks like:
- Cosigner release options
- Unemployment protection
- Rate discounts for autopay

4. Evaluate Your Creditworthiness

If your credit score isn’t strong enough, consider applying with a cosigner to secure better rates.

When Refinancing Might Not Be the Best Choice

While refinancing can save money, it’s not always the right move. Avoid refinancing if:

  • You have federal loans – Losing access to IDR plans or Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) could be costly.
  • Your credit score is low – You may not qualify for better rates than your current loans.
  • You’re close to loan forgiveness – Refinancing resets the clock on forgiveness programs.

Steps to Refinance Your Undergraduate Student Loans

1. Check Your Credit Score

Most lenders require a score of at least 650. Use free tools like Credit Karma or Experian to review yours.

2. Gather Necessary Documents

  • Loan statements
  • Proof of income (pay stubs, tax returns)
  • Employment verification

3. Prequalify with Multiple Lenders

Many lenders offer soft credit checks that don’t affect your score. Compare offers before applying.

4. Submit a Formal Application

Once you choose a lender, complete the full application. Approval can take a few days to weeks.

5. Continue Making Payments Until Refinancing is Finalized

Don’t stop paying your old loans until the new lender confirms the refinance is complete.

Alternative Strategies to Manage Student Debt

If refinancing isn’t the right fit, consider:

  • Federal Loan Consolidation – Combines federal loans into one without losing benefits.
  • Income-Driven Repayment Plans – Caps payments at a percentage of your income.
  • Employer Student Loan Assistance – Some companies offer repayment benefits.

Student loan refinancing can be a powerful tool for undergraduates looking to take control of their debt. By researching lenders, comparing rates, and understanding your financial situation, you can find the best option to save money and achieve financial freedom.
