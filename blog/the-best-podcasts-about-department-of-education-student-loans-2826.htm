The Best Podcasts About Department of Education Student Loans

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Student loans are a hot-button issue in today’s world, affecting millions of borrowers across the United States. With rising tuition costs, fluctuating interest rates, and ever-changing federal policies, staying informed is crucial. Podcasts have become an invaluable resource for breaking down complex topics like Department of Education (DOE) student loans in an engaging and digestible way. Whether you're a recent graduate, a parent helping with college financing, or a policy enthusiast, these podcasts offer expert insights, actionable advice, and deep dives into the latest developments.

Why Listen to Podcasts About Student Loans?

Podcasts provide a unique blend of education and entertainment, making them perfect for busy individuals who want to stay updated on the go. Here’s why tuning into student loan podcasts is a smart move:

  • Expert Interviews: Many podcasts feature financial advisors, loan servicers, and policymakers who share insider knowledge.
  • Policy Updates: The DOE frequently changes repayment plans, forgiveness programs, and interest rates—podcasts keep you ahead of the curve.
  • Personal Stories: Hearing real-life experiences from borrowers can help you navigate your own loan journey.
  • Free & Accessible: Unlike paid financial advisors, podcasts offer free advice at your fingertips.

Top Podcasts About DOE Student Loans

1. The Student Loan Podcast

Hosted by financial expert Drew Cloud, The Student Loan Podcast is a must-listen for anyone dealing with federal or private student loans. Each episode tackles pressing topics like:

  • Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Plans: How to choose the best one for your financial situation.
  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF): Common mistakes that disqualify applicants and how to avoid them.
  • Biden’s Student Loan Forgiveness Updates: Breaking down executive actions and legislative proposals.

Cloud often invites DOE representatives and loan servicers to clarify confusing policies, making this podcast a reliable source for accurate information.

2. Student Debt Crisis Center’s Debt Free Degree

The Student Debt Crisis Center (SDCC) is a nonprofit advocating for fair student loan policies. Their podcast, Debt Free Degree, focuses on systemic issues and borrower advocacy. Key episodes include:

  • The True Cost of Student Loans: How debt impacts mental health, homeownership, and retirement savings.
  • Navigating Default: Steps to rehabilitate defaulted loans and rebuild credit.
  • State vs. Federal Loans: Differences in forgiveness programs and repayment options.

This podcast is ideal for listeners who want to understand the broader economic and social implications of student debt.

3. The College Investor Show

Robert Farrington, founder of The College Investor, hosts this podcast dedicated to smart financial decisions for students and graduates. While it covers broader personal finance topics, it frequently dives into DOE loans with episodes like:

  • Refinancing Federal Loans: Pros and Cons – When it makes sense (and when it doesn’t).
  • Parent PLUS Loans: What Every Cosigner Should Know – Avoiding pitfalls that lead to long-term debt.
  • Scams Targeting Student Loan Borrowers – How to spot fraudulent forgiveness programs.

Farrington’s straightforward advice makes complex topics easy to understand.

4. NPR’s Life Kit: Money

NPR’s Life Kit series includes a money-focused segment that frequently covers student loans. Their well-researched episodes feature:

  • How to Appeal for Lower Monthly Payments – Negotiating with loan servicers.
  • The Future of Student Loan Forgiveness – Analyzing political and legal challenges.
  • Alternatives to Traditional Student Loans – Exploring scholarships, work-study, and employer tuition assistance.

With NPR’s signature journalistic rigor, this podcast delivers trustworthy, unbiased information.

5. The Dave Ramsey Show

While Dave Ramsey is best known for his anti-debt philosophy, his show occasionally addresses student loans with a no-nonsense approach. Highlights include:

  • The Snowball Method for Student Loans – Paying off debt fastest while staying motivated.
  • Why Borrowing for College Can Be a Bad Idea – Exploring trade schools and community colleges.
  • When to Prioritize Student Loans Over Other Debts – Balancing loans with mortgages and credit cards.

Ramsey’s tough-love style isn’t for everyone, but his strategies work for those committed to aggressive repayment.

Emerging Trends in Student Loans

The Rise of Loan Forgiveness Programs

With the Biden administration’s push for widespread debt relief, podcasts are buzzing about:

  • Expanded PSLF Waivers – Temporary changes helping more borrowers qualify.
  • One-Time Account Adjustments – How past payments may count toward forgiveness.
  • Legal Battles Over Debt Cancellation – Updates on lawsuits blocking relief efforts.

Private Loans vs. Federal Loans

Many borrowers don’t realize federal loans offer protections private lenders don’t, such as:

  • Deferment and Forbearance Options – Pausing payments during financial hardship.
  • Death and Disability Discharge – Federal loans are forgiven in these cases; private loans often aren’t.
  • Lower Fixed Interest Rates – Private loans can have variable rates that skyrocket over time.

The Mental Health Impact

Podcasts are increasingly covering how student debt contributes to:

  • Delayed Life Milestones – Postponing marriage, kids, or homebuying due to debt.
  • Anxiety and Depression – The psychological toll of long-term financial stress.
  • Coping Strategies – Budgeting, therapy, and support groups for borrowers.

How to Get the Most Out of These Podcasts

  • Take Notes: Jot down key deadlines, repayment strategies, or contact info for loan servicers.
  • Follow Up with Official Resources: Cross-check podcast advice with the DOE’s website (studentaid.gov).
  • Engage with Communities: Many podcasts have Facebook groups or subreddits where listeners share tips.

By staying informed through these podcasts, you’ll be better equipped to manage your student loans and advocate for fair policies. Whether you’re looking for repayment hacks, policy deep dives, or emotional support, there’s a podcast out there for you.
