Veterans United Home Loans: Common Questions Answered

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Navigating the world of home loans can be overwhelming, especially for veterans and active-duty service members who have unique benefits available to them. Veterans United Home Loans specializes in helping military families secure financing through VA loans, but many borrowers still have questions about the process, eligibility, and current market trends. Below, we address some of the most common inquiries while tying in today’s pressing economic and housing challenges.

Understanding VA Loans in Today’s Housing Market

What Is a VA Loan, and How Does It Work?

A VA loan is a mortgage backed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, designed to help veterans, active-duty service members, and eligible surviving spouses become homeowners. Unlike conventional loans, VA loans often require no down payment, have competitive interest rates, and don’t mandate private mortgage insurance (PMI).

In today’s high-interest-rate environment, VA loans remain an attractive option because they typically offer lower rates than conventional mortgages. With housing affordability at a crisis point in many parts of the U.S., this benefit can make homeownership achievable for military families who might otherwise be priced out.

Who Qualifies for a VA Loan?

Eligibility is determined by service history. Generally, you qualify if you:
- Served 90 consecutive days during wartime
- Served 181 days during peacetime
- Have six years of service in the National Guard or Reserves
- Are the surviving spouse of a service member who died in the line of duty

Given the rising number of veterans transitioning to civilian life post-Afghanistan and Iraq, understanding these benefits is crucial. Many veterans aren’t aware they qualify, leaving money-saving opportunities untapped.

Current Challenges in the Housing Market

Rising Interest Rates and Inflation

The Federal Reserve’s aggressive rate hikes to combat inflation have pushed mortgage rates to two-decade highs. For veterans, this makes the VA loan’s lower rates even more valuable. However, some buyers worry about locking in a rate now versus waiting for potential drops.

While timing the market is nearly impossible, VA loans offer the option to refinance later through the Interest Rate Reduction Refinance Loan (IRRRL), providing flexibility if rates decline.

Low Housing Inventory

The U.S. faces a severe housing shortage, with estimates suggesting a deficit of 3.8 million homes. For VA loan users, this can mean tougher competition, especially since some sellers still hold misconceptions about VA loans (e.g., they’re slower or more complicated).

Veterans United works to educate sellers and agents, emphasizing that VA loans close just as quickly as conventional ones when processed efficiently.

Common Misconceptions About VA Loans

“VA Loans Take Too Long to Close”

This myth persists despite data showing VA loans now close in about 50 days—on par with conventional loans. Delays usually stem from appraisal backlogs or incomplete paperwork, not the loan type itself.

“You Can Only Use a VA Loan Once”

False. Veterans can reuse their VA loan benefit multiple times, either by paying off the previous loan or selling the home (and paying it off in the process). Remaining entitlement can also be restored.

“VA Loans Are Only for First-Time Buyers”

There’s no restriction on how many times you can use a VA loan, whether you’re a first-time or repeat buyer. This flexibility is especially helpful in today’s market, where military families may need to relocate frequently due to PCS orders.

How Veterans United Stands Out

Personalized Support for Military Families

Unlike many lenders, Veterans United employs loan officers who specialize in VA loans and understand military life. This expertise is critical when navigating frequent moves, deployment-related challenges, or complex entitlement situations.

Streamlined Digital Process

The company’s online tools, like the VA Loan Calculator and pre-approval portal, simplify the borrowing process—a major advantage when competing in fast-moving markets.

Addressing Today’s Economic Pressures

With inflation squeezing budgets, Veterans United offers resources like financial coaching to help veterans manage debt and improve credit before applying.

FAQs: Quick Answers to Veterans’ Top Concerns

Can I Use a VA Loan for an Investment Property?

No. VA loans are strictly for primary residences, though you can rent out unused rooms or later convert the home into a rental.

What’s the Funding Fee, and Can It Be Waived?

The VA funding fee (a one-time charge) ranges from 1.25% to 3.3% of the loan amount. It’s waived for veterans with service-connected disabilities or those receiving VA compensation.

How Does the VA Appraisal Work?

The VA requires an appraisal to ensure the home meets Minimum Property Requirements (MPRs). In tight markets, buyers should be prepared for possible repair negotiations.

Looking Ahead: VA Loans in 2024 and Beyond

As remote work reshapes housing demand, more veterans are considering relocation to affordable areas. VA loans’ zero-down feature makes this feasible even as prices rise. Meanwhile, legislative efforts to expand VA loan benefits—like higher entitlement limits—could further ease access.

For military families facing today’s economic headwinds, VA loans remain a powerful tool. By debunking myths and staying informed, veterans can confidently pursue homeownership despite market volatility.
